## Anatomy-as-Evidence Principle

Anatomical sections are not inventories of parts.

They are **scientific analyses** showing how structure:

- enables biological function  
- varies among taxa  
- supports classification  

---

## Core Objective

Build exhaustive anatomy sections covering:

Head → Thorax → Abdomen  

with each structure analyzed through:

morphology + function + variation + taxonomic value.

---

## Mandatory Structural Domains

### Head

Must include:

- mouthparts (labrum, mandibles, maxillae, labium, palps)  
- compound eyes and ocelli  
- antennae (types and segmentation)  

For each:

- precise morphology  
- feeding/sensory function  
- inter-order variation  
- classification relevance  

---

### Thorax

Must include:

- prothorax, mesothorax, metathorax  
- wing attachment and specialization  
- leg structure and modifications  

For each:

- segmental role  
- locomotor function  
- adaptive modifications  
- taxonomic diagnostic traits  

---

### Abdomen

Must include:

- segmentation and sclerites (tergites, sternites)  
- respiratory openings (spiracles)  
- reproductive structures (ovipositor, aedeagus, cerci)  

For each:

- physiological role  
- structural variation  
- identification significance  

---

## Function–Variation Coupling

Every anatomical feature must address:

- what it does biologically  
- how it differs across taxa  
- why those differences matter scientifically  

---

## Taxonomic Evidence Emphasis

Structures must be explicitly used to:

- identify orders and families  
- distinguish close relatives  
- justify classification decisions  

---

## Scientific Terminology Enforcement

Use:

- formal anatomical vocabulary  
- precise morphological descriptors  
- accepted entomological nomenclature  

Avoid:

- casual language  
- oversimplification  
- unlabeled generalizations  

---

## Integration with Figures Encouraged

Where applicable:

- link structures to labeled visuals  
- reference diagrams as anatomical evidence  

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- listing parts without interpretation  
- describing function without variation  
- ignoring classification value  
- fragmenting anatomical logic  

---

## Mental Model for the AI Agent

You are writing a textbook-quality anatomical analysis for scientific study.

Your goal is to reveal how insect body design encodes taxonomy and function.

---

## Quality Threshold

A successful anatomy section:

- is comprehensive  
- is functionally explained  
- shows variation clearly  
- supports classification rigorously  

---

## Default Priority Order

1. Anatomical completeness  
2. Functional explanation  
3. Taxonomic relevance  
4. Morphological accuracy  
5. Scientific clarity
