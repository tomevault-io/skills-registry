# Analytics & Measurement

## Overview
This skill provides a framework for implementing best practices in marketing analytics and measurement, helping you focus on what matters and avoid common pitfalls.

**Keywords**: analytics, measurement, marketing analytics, data analysis, conversion tracking, ROI

## Discovery & Planning Questions
- What are the primary business objectives you want your marketing to achieve? (e.g., increase sales, generate leads, build brand awareness)
- Who is your target audience? Can you describe your ideal customer?
- What are the most important actions you want visitors to take on your website? (e.g., make a purchase, fill out a contact form, subscribe to a newsletter)
- What marketing channels are you currently using? (e.g., SEO, social media, email marketing, paid ads)
- What analytics tools, if any, do you currently have in place? (e.g., Google Analytics, a CRM, etc.)
- What is your timeline for seeing results from your marketing efforts?
- Are there any specific metrics or KPIs you are currently tracking?
- What does success look like for you in the next 90 days?
- Do you have any existing reports or dashboards? What do you like or dislike about them?

## Instructions
When this skill is active, your primary goal is to help the user make data-driven decisions to improve their marketing performance. You should guide them through the process of setting clear goals, tracking the right metrics, and analyzing their data to uncover actionable insights.

## S-Tier Tactics (Must-Do)
- **Define SMART Goals**: Guide the user to set Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound goals for their marketing campaigns.
- **Focus on Actionable Metrics**: Help the user differentiate between vanity metrics and actionable metrics that tie directly to their business goals.
- **Implement a Centralized Analytics Platform**: Recommend and guide the user in setting up a centralized analytics platform like Google Analytics to track website traffic, user behavior, and conversions.
- **Track Conversions**: Assist the user in identifying and tracking key conversion actions on their website, such as purchases, form submissions, or newsletter sign-ups.

## A-Tier Tactics (Highly Effective)
- **A/B Testing**: Encourage and help the user to run A/B tests on their webpages, emails, and other marketing assets to optimize for conversions.
- **Customer Journey Tracking & Multi-Touch Attribution**: Help the user understand the different touchpoints a customer has with their brand and implement a multi-touch attribution model to accurately measure the impact of each channel.
- **Audience Segmentation**: Guide the user in segmenting their audience based on demographics, behavior, or interests to deliver more personalized and effective marketing messages.
- **Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM)**: For users with more complex marketing mixes, introduce the concept of MMM to help them understand the impact of their various marketing tactics on sales and optimize their marketing spend.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid (D-Tier)
- **Focusing Solely on Last-Click Attribution**: Advise the user against relying solely on last-click attribution and explain the importance of understanding the entire customer journey.
- **Ignoring Data Quality**: Emphasize the importance of data quality and guide the user on how to ensure their data is accurate, complete, and consistent.
- **Not Having a Measurement Plan**: Stress the need for a measurement plan that outlines their business objectives, strategies, and KPIs.

## Examples

**Example 1: Setting up conversion tracking**
*User: "I want to track how many people sign up for my newsletter."
*Claude: "Great! That's a valuable conversion to track. To get started, we'll need to create a 'Thank You' page that users are redirected to after they sign up. Then, we'll set up a goal in Google Analytics to track how many people land on that page. This will give us a clear picture of how many newsletter sign-ups you're getting and where they're coming from."

**Example 2: A/B testing a headline**
*User: "My landing page isn't converting very well. What can I do?"
*Claude: "One effective strategy is to A/B test your headline. We can create a second version of your landing page with a different headline and then show each version to 50% of your visitors. After a week or two, we can analyze the data to see which headline resulted in more conversions. This will help us make a data-driven decision to improve your page's performance."

## Workflow
1. **Goal Setting**: Start by defining the user's business objectives and translating them into SMART marketing goals.
2. **Measurement Plan**: Create a measurement plan that outlines the key performance indicators (KPIs) for each goal.
3. **Implementation**: Guide the user in implementing the necessary tracking and analytics tools.
4. **Data Collection**: Ensure data is being collected accurately and consistently.
5. **Analysis**: Analyze the data to identify trends, patterns, and insights.
6. **Optimization**: Provide actionable recommendations for optimizing marketing campaigns based on the analysis.
7. **Reporting**: Help the user create reports and dashboards to communicate the results to stakeholders.
