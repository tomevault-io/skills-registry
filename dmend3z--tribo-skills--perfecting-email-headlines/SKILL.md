# Email Headline Perfecter

## Before We Begin

To provide the most effective subject lines, this skill first needs to understand the specific context. Please answer the following questions:

### Discovery Questions

1.  What is the specific product, service, or message being promoted in this email?
2.  Describe the target audience. What are their primary pain points, desires, and level of awareness regarding the brand?
3.  What is the primary goal of this email (e.g., drive sales, increase webinar sign-ups, share news, nurture leads)?
4.  What is the typical email open rate, and what has been tried in the past with subject lines?
5.  Are there any brand voice guidelines, keywords, or specific phrases that must be included or avoided?
6.  What is the context of this email? Is it part of a sequence, a one-off broadcast, or a transactional message?
7.  What is the single most important value or piece of information the reader should take away from this email?

## What This Skill Does

This skill serves as an expert subject line strategist, moving beyond generic templates to engineer email subject lines that demand attention in a crowded inbox. By understanding the intricate details of the audience, offer, and objectives, it constructs a series of subject line variations designed for maximum impact. The core purpose is not just to suggest text, but to provide a strategic framework for email marketing efforts.

The approach is rooted in a blend of proven copywriting principles and psychological insights. It analyzes the emotional and logical triggers that compel a person to open an email, ensuring that each suggestion is tailored to the specific context. This skill helps break free from uninspired subject lines that get ignored, providing creative, data-informed alternatives that spark curiosity and build momentum for the message.

## Core Methodology

This skill utilizes a multi-faceted approach to develop subject lines that are both creative and effective. Each framework is applied to generate a diverse set of options, providing a range of strategic choices.

### The 4-U Engagement Model
This foundational framework ensures every subject line has a clear purpose. Each option is evaluated to ensure it is **Useful** (offers clear value), **Urgent** (encourages immediate action), **Unique** (stands out from the competition), and **Ultra-specific** (provides compelling detail).

### Psychological Trigger Mapping
By analyzing the audience and goals, this skill maps subject lines to core psychological drivers. This includes crafting options that leverage curiosity, social proof, scarcity, authority, and personal connection to create a deeper resonance with the reader.

### Predictive Performance Modeling
Instead of offering a vague open rate percentage, this skill provides a comparative analysis. Each subject line variation is given a performance score based on its alignment with the core methodologies, along with a qualitative explanation of its strengths and potential weaknesses.

## Advanced Capabilities

This skill goes beyond the basics with these expert-level features:

### Emotional Resonance Analysis
Each subject line is scored based on its potential to evoke a specific emotional response. This feature identifies whether a subject line inspires curiosity, excitement, urgency, or trust, allowing the tone of the subject line to be aligned with the goal of the email campaign.

### Personalization Vectoring
This capability provides concrete recommendations for advanced personalization. Moving beyond a simple `[First Name]` token, it suggests ways to incorporate audience-specific data—such as their industry, past behaviors, or stated interests—directly into the subject line to create a powerful one-to-one connection.

### Spam Filter Pre-Check
To improve deliverability, this skill automatically analyzes generated subject lines for common spam triggers. It flags problematic words (e.g., "free," "winner"), excessive capitalization, and misleading formatting that could land an email in the spam folder, providing safer alternatives.

## How to Use This Skill

To use this skill, provide an existing subject line or the core idea for the email. The skill will then ask the discovery questions to gather context. Once the answers are provided, it will perform its analysis and generate a set of optimized subject line variations with a detailed breakdown of the strategy behind each one.

### Example Prompts

- "Generate three subject lines for a new product launch email targeting busy professionals."
- "Optimize this subject line for a flash sale announcement: '24-Hour Sale Starts Now'"
- "Create subject lines for a lead nurturing sequence aimed at new subscribers who downloaded our e-book."

## Output Format

The skill will deliver a report containing 3-5 optimized subject line variations. For each variation, a detailed analysis will be provided that includes its Predictive Performance Score, the core psychological triggers it uses, and specific recommendations for implementation and testing.
