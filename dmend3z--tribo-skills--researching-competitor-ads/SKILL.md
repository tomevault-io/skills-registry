# Competitor Ad Researcher

> Gain a decisive edge by dissecting competitor ad strategies and creative.

## Before We Begin

I need to understand your specific situation before I can help effectively. Please answer these questions:

### Discovery Questions

1. What product or service are you advertising, and what is its unique value proposition?
2. Who is your ideal customer, and what are their primary pain points or desires that your product addresses?
3. What is your primary objective for this analysis? (e.g., improve ad copy, discover new creative angles, understand competitor funnels)
4. Please list 3-5 of your top competitors whose advertising you wish to analyze.
5. Are there specific advertising platforms (e.g., Facebook, Google, TikTok, LinkedIn) you are most interested in?
6. What is your approximate monthly advertising budget? This helps to contextualize the scale and potential reach of your competitors' campaigns.
7. Have you conducted any competitor ad analysis in the past? If so, what were your key findings or limitations?

## What This Skill Does

This skill provides a deep-dive analysis of your competitors' advertising strategies. It moves beyond surface-level imitation by systematically deconstructing the core components of their campaigns—from the psychological triggers in their copy to the visual language of their creatives. The goal is to equip you with a strategic understanding of what's working in your market, why it's working, and how you can carve out a unique and compelling space for your own brand.

By examining the messaging, offers, and creative formats your rivals are using, you can identify patterns of success, uncover gaps in the market, and gather inspiration for your own campaigns. This process is not about copying but about strategic intelligence. It allows you to learn from the collective experiments and investments of others, enabling you to make more informed, effective, and capital-efficient decisions in your own advertising efforts.

## Core Methodology

This skill employs a structured framework to ensure a comprehensive and insightful analysis.

### Creative Reconnaissance
This initial phase involves a systematic sweep and collection of ad creatives from your specified competitors across various ad libraries and platforms. We gather a broad sample of ads to ensure the analysis is based on a comprehensive dataset, not just a few isolated examples.

### Narrative Deconstruction
Here, we dissect the language and messaging of each ad. This involves identifying the core value propositions being highlighted, the emotional triggers being pulled (e.g., fear, hope, curiosity), and the overall story being told. We analyze headlines, body copy, and calls-to-action to understand how your competitors are speaking to their audience.

### Strategic Grouping
We categorize the collected ads based on key strategic dimensions. This could include grouping by the offer being presented (e.g., discount, free trial, lead magnet), the stage of the customer funnel being targeted (e.g., top-of-funnel awareness vs. bottom-of-funnel conversion), or the core marketing angle being used.

### Insight Synthesis
In the final step, we bring all the pieces together to identify high-level patterns and actionable insights. This involves pinpointing recurring successful formulas, identifying dominant messaging themes in the industry, and highlighting strategic opportunities or 
unexploited angles for your brand.

## Advanced Capabilities

This skill goes beyond the basics with these expert-level features:

### Funnel Mapping
We don't just look at the ads themselves; we analyze the entire user journey. This involves clicking through the ads to map out the landing pages, sales funnels, and retargeting sequences that your competitors are using. This provides a much deeper understanding of their overall conversion strategy.

### Emotional Resonance Analysis
Using sentiment analysis and psycholinguistic frameworks, we assess the underlying emotional tone of competitor ad copy. This reveals whether they are leading with fear, aspiration, urgency, or other emotions, helping you to position your own messaging in a way that stands out.
