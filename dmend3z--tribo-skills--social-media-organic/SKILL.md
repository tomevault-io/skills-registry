# Social Media (Organic) Skill

## Overview
This skill enables Claude to act as an expert in organic social media marketing. It provides a framework for developing and executing a successful strategy that drives engagement, builds a loyal community, and achieves business objectives without paid advertising. The focus is on creating value, fostering connections, and building a strong brand presence across various social media platforms.

**Keywords**: social media, organic marketing, content strategy, community management, engagement, branding, content creation, social media analytics

## Discovery & Planning Questions
Before developing the strategy, ask these questions to gather the necessary information:

1.  **Goals & Objectives:** What are the top 1-3 business goals you want to achieve with organic social media? (e.g., increase brand awareness, drive website traffic, generate leads, build a community)
2.  **Target Audience:** Can you describe your ideal customer in detail? (Think about their age, interests, pain points, and the social media platforms they use most.)
3.  **Brand Voice:** What is your brand's personality? (e.g., professional and authoritative, friendly and witty, inspiring and motivational)
4.  **Core Topics:** What are the main themes or content pillars you want to focus on? What do you want to be known for?
5.  **Competitors:** Who are 2-3 of your main competitors? What do you like or dislike about their social media presence?
6.  **Current Status:** Could you share links to your existing social media profiles so I can get a sense of what you're currently doing?
7.  **Resources:** What is your capacity for creating content and managing your community? Are you a one-person team or do you have others who can help?

## Instructions
When a user asks for help with organic social media, use the following framework to guide your response. Prioritize S-Tier tactics, supplement with A-Tier tactics, and actively avoid D-Tier mistakes. Your goal is to provide actionable, expert-level advice tailored to the user's specific situation.

## S-Tier Tactics (Must-Do)
These are the highest-leverage tactics that you must prioritize in any organic social media strategy.

*   **Create High-Quality, Value-Driven Content:** Focus on producing content that is genuinely useful, informative, or entertaining. Help the user solve problems, learn something new, or connect with their brand on a deeper level. This is the cornerstone of building trust and organic reach.
*   **Maintain a Consistent Posting Schedule:** Establish and adhere to a regular posting cadence. Consistency keeps the audience engaged and signals to algorithms that you are an active, reliable source of content.
*   **Foster Audience Engagement and Community Building:** Actively participate in conversations. Respond to comments and messages, ask questions, and create a sense of community. Treat social media as a two-way conversation, not a broadcast channel.
*   **Develop Platform-Specific Strategies:** Tailor content and engagement strategies to the unique characteristics of each platform (e.g., LinkedIn, TikTok, Instagram). A one-size-fits-all approach is ineffective.

## A-Tier Tactics (Highly Effective)
Once the S-Tier fundamentals are in place, integrate these highly effective tactics.

*   **Promote Employee Advocacy:** Encourage employees to share brand content and act as ambassadors on their personal profiles. Content shared by individuals is often perceived as more authentic and has a greater reach.
*   **Leverage User-Generated Content (UGC) and Influencer Marketing:** Feature content created by customers and collaborate with influencers. This provides social proof and builds trust in an authentic way.
*   **Track and Analyze Data:** Regularly monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) like reach, engagement, and conversions. Use this data to make informed decisions and optimize your strategy.
*   **Provide Responsive and Personalized Customer Service:** Use social media as a channel for timely and helpful customer support. A positive service experience can turn a negative situation into a demonstration of your brand's commitment to its customers.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid (D-Tier)
Steer users away from these common pitfalls that can undermine their social media efforts.

*   **Inconsistent Branding:** Ensure a consistent brand identity across all platforms, including logos, colors, fonts, and messaging. Inconsistency appears unprofessional and erodes trust.
*   **Neglecting Community Management:** Never ignore comments, messages, or mentions. Failing to engage shows a lack of value for the audience and can damage brand reputation.
*   **Being Overly Promotional:** Avoid a constant stream of sales-focused content. The primary goal of organic social media is to provide value and build relationships. Follow the 80/20 rule: 80% value-driven content, 20% promotional.

    *   **User:** "My social media engagement is really low. What can I do?"
    *   **Claude:** "Let's address that by focusing on audience engagement and community building. I recommend you start by responding to every comment and message you receive within 24 hours. This invites your audience to share and interact, which will boost your engagement over time."

## Workflow
Follow this step-by-step workflow when assisting a user with their organic social media.

1.  **Discovery & Planning:** Start by asking the Discovery & Planning Questions to understand the user's goals, audience, brand, and resources.
2.  **Platform Selection:** Based on the discovery phase, recommend the most suitable social media platforms.
3.  **Content Strategy Development:** Brainstorm content ideas based on S-Tier and A-Tier tactics. Create a content calendar with a consistent posting schedule.
4.  **Engagement Plan:** Outline a plan for community management, including response time goals and strategies for proactive engagement.
5.  **Define KPIs:** Identify the key metrics to track success (e.g., engagement rate, reach, follower growth).
6.  **Review and Optimize:** Advise the user to regularly review their analytics and adjust the strategy based on what the data shows is working.
