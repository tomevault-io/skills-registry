# Website Management

## Overview
This skill provides a comprehensive framework for managing a website effectively, covering critical areas from security and performance to SEO and user experience. By following these best practices, you can help users maintain a healthy, high-performing, and secure online presence.

**Keywords**: website management, website security, website performance, SEO, mobile optimization, backups, Core Web Vitals

## Discovery & Planning Questions
1.  **Goals & Objectives:** What is the primary purpose of your website (e.g., generate leads, sell products, provide information)? What are your main goals for this management/improvement project?
2.  **Target Audience:** Who are you trying to reach with your website? Please describe your ideal visitor or customer.
3.  **Technical Foundation:** What platform is your website built on (e.g., WordPress, Shopify, Squarespace), and who is your hosting provider?
4.  **Current Challenges:** What are the biggest pain points or challenges you're currently facing with your website (e.g., slow speed, low traffic, security concerns, poor mobile experience)?
5.  **Analytics & Insights:** Do you have any analytics tools installed, like Google Analytics or Google Search Console? What have you learned from them so far?
6.  **Team & Resources:** What is your level of technical expertise, and how much time can you dedicate to managing your site each week?
7.  **Competitive Landscape:** Can you share 1-2 competitors? What do you like or dislike about their websites?

## Instructions
When a user asks for help with their website, first ask the Discovery & Planning Questions to assess their goals and the current state of their site. Inquire about their current security measures, backup frequency, mobile experience, and performance metrics. Then, guide them through the S-Tier and A-Tier tactics to build a strong foundation. Finally, help them establish a routine for ongoing maintenance and improvement.

## S-Tier Tactics (Must-Do)
- **Implement Robust Website Security:** Ensure the use of SSL certificates, strong passwords, firewalls, and regular security scans to protect against malware and hacking.
- **Automate Frequent Backups:** Set up automated, regular backups of website files and databases, stored in a separate, secure location.
- **Prioritize Mobile-First Optimization:** Guarantee a seamless experience on mobile devices with fast loading times, easy navigation, and touch-friendly design.
- **Optimize for Core Web Vitals & Performance:** Focus on improving loading speed, interactivity, and visual stability by compressing images, leveraging caching, and minimizing code.

## A-Tier Tactics (Highly Effective)
- **Maintain Consistent Software & Plugin Updates:** Regularly update the CMS, plugins, and themes to their latest versions to patch security vulnerabilities and improve performance.
- **Implement Foundational SEO:** Apply on-page and technical SEO best practices, including keyword research, optimizing meta tags, and ensuring the site is crawlable.
- **Utilize Website Analytics & Monitoring:** Set up and monitor tools like Google Analytics and Google Search Console to track traffic, user behavior, and keyword performance. Use uptime monitoring to receive alerts if the site goes down.
- **Create Clear & Compelling Calls-to-Action (CTAs):** Strategically place persuasive CTAs to guide users towards desired actions like making a purchase or filling out a form.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid (D-Tier)
- **Do Not Neglect the Mobile Experience:** Never focus solely on the desktop version. A poor mobile experience will alienate the majority of users and harm search rankings.
- **Do Not Ignore Security & Update Warnings:** Always address notifications about software updates and security vulnerabilities promptly.
- **Avoid Keyword Stuffing:** Do not overload content with keywords. Write high-quality, natural-sounding content that provides value to the user.
- **Avoid Using Obtrusive Pop-ups & Ads:** Do not bombard users with intrusive pop-ups or an excessive number of ads. Use them sparingly and ensure they are easy to dismiss.

"To improve your Google rankings, we need to focus on foundational SEO. I'll help you with keyword research to understand what your audience is searching for. Then, we can optimize your title tags, meta descriptions, and on-page content to make your site more visible in search results."

## Workflow
1.  **Assess the Website:** Begin by asking the Discovery & Planning questions and performing a high-level audit of the user's website, focusing on the S-Tier and A-Tier tactics.
2.  **Prioritize Actions:** Based on the audit, create a prioritized action plan, addressing the most critical issues first.
3.  **Implement S-Tier Tactics:** Guide the user through implementing robust security, automated backups, mobile-first optimization, and performance improvements.
4.  **Implement A-Tier Tactics:** Once the foundation is solid, move on to implementing foundational SEO, setting up analytics, and optimizing CTAs.
5.  **Establish a Maintenance Routine:** Help the user create a schedule for regular software updates, content updates, and website audits to ensure the long-term health of the site.
