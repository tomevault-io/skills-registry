# Character Design

## Purpose

Create characters that feel like real people. Every character must be distinguishable from every other character by personality, speech, and worldview alone -- without needing physical descriptions.

## Workflow

1. **Read existing materials** -- Read the world bible and any existing character sheets for this book
2. **Gather input** -- Ask the user for the character's role, any seeds they have in mind
3. **Generate the character sheet** using the template at `templates/character-sheet.md`
4. **Differentiation check** -- Compare the new character against all existing characters and flag any overlaps
5. **Write to file** at `books/<book-title>/characters/<character-name>.md`
6. **Review with user** -- Present highlights and ask for revisions

## The Uniqueness Mandate

Before finalizing any character, answer these questions:

1. If I removed all names and physical descriptions, could a reader tell this character apart from every other character in the cast? **If no, redesign.**
2. Does this character want something that puts them in conflict with at least one other character? **If no, add tension.**
3. Does this character have a flaw that is not just "too brave" or "too loyal"? **If no, dig deeper.**
4. Would this character make a different decision than the protagonist in the same situation? **If no, differentiate.**

## Personality Construction Method

Build personality from the inside out:

### Layer 1: Core Drive

What does this person want more than anything? This is not a plot goal -- it is a psychological need. Examples: control, belonging, recognition, safety, freedom.

### Layer 2: Wound

What happened that made them need this so badly? A specific event, not a vague backstory.

### Layer 3: Mask

How do they present themselves to hide or cope with the wound? The mask is what other characters see first.

### Layer 4: Contradiction

Where does their behavior contradict their stated values? This is where depth lives.

### Layer 5: Breaking Point

What would it take to shatter the mask and expose the wound? This is their arc climax.

## Speech Pattern Design

Every character needs a distinct voice. Define:

- **Sentence length tendency** -- Short and punchy? Long and winding? Mixed?
- **Vocabulary source** -- Where did they learn to talk? Military? Academia? Streets? Rural?
- **Default emotional register** -- Sarcastic? Earnest? Guarded? Performative?
- **Tics and habits** -- Do they repeat themselves? Trail off? Use specific filler words?
- **What they never say** -- What topics or words does this character avoid?

## Relationship Mapping

For every character, define their relationship to every other existing character:

- What they think of the other person
- What the other person thinks of them
- The unspoken tension between them
- How they behave differently around this person vs. others

## Validation Checklist

- [ ] Character passes the Uniqueness Mandate (all 4 questions)
- [ ] All 5 personality layers are filled in
- [ ] Speech pattern is concretely defined with examples
- [ ] At least one relationship creates active tension
- [ ] Fatal flaw is specific and non-generic
- [ ] Conscious desire and unconscious need are different things
- [ ] The character sheet is written to `books/<book-title>/characters/`
