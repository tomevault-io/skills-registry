## Pre-Outreach Research

Before writing any email, spend 5 minutes finding the one thing that makes this prospect feel seen. Generic outreach gets deleted. Specific outreach gets replies.

Look for:
- **Recent signal** — New hire, funding round, product launch, job posting, website update, press mention
- **Pain evidence** — Review complaints, G2/Capterra comments, support forum posts, LinkedIn posts venting about processes
- **Fit indicators** — Company size, tech stack (check BuiltWith), role of the contact, revenue signals
- **Warm connection** — Mutual connection, shared community, you've used their product

The goal: one specific sentence that proves you looked. Not flattery — evidence.

## Discovery Call Guide

A discovery call is about listening, not pitching. Your goal is to understand the problem well enough to propose the right solution and the right price.

### Call Structure (30 minutes)

**Opening (2 min)** — Set the agenda
> "I want to make sure this is useful for you. I'll ask a few questions about your current setup, then share what we typically do for companies like yours. Sound good?"

**Discovery (15 min)** — Ask these in order:
1. "Walk me through how [the process they contacted you about] works today."
2. "Where does it break down or take the most time?"
3. "What have you tried to fix it? What happened?"
4. "What would it mean for your team if this was solved?"
5. "Who else is involved in decisions like this?"

**Solution fit (8 min)** — Reflect back what you heard, then connect it to your service:
> "So it sounds like the main problem is [X], and you've tried [Y] but it didn't work because [Z]. We typically solve this by [brief description]. We've done this for [similar company type] and they saw [concrete result]."

**Next step (5 min)** — Never leave without a next step:
> "Based on what you've told me, I think there's a fit. I'll put together a proposal by [specific date]. Does [time] on [day] work to walk through it together?"

### What to Capture From Every Call
- Current process (step by step)
- Where the biggest pain/time loss is
- What they've tried before
- Decision makers and budget signals
- Timeline and urgency
- Their words for the problem (use these in the proposal)

## Closing Tactics

Deals stall because of unresolved risk, not price. When someone says "let me think about it," something is unresolved — your job is to surface it.

### Identifying the Real Stall

Ask: "Totally fair — what would help me make this an easier yes?"

Common answers and how to respond:

| What They Say | What's Really Happening | Response |
|---|---|---|
| "Need to think about it" | Unresolved concern | "What's the biggest thing on your mind right now?" |
| "Need to check with [person]" | Wrong stakeholder on the call | "Should we set up a call with them this week? Happy to re-present." |
| "Budget is tight" | Not convinced on ROI | Run the ROI math together (see Objection Handling) |
| "Not the right time" | Low urgency | Quantify the cost of waiting: "Each month is roughly $X in admin time" |
| "Let me talk to other vendors" | Not sold on differentiation | "What would you need to see from us to move forward without shopping around?" |

### Soft Close Phrasing
After sending a proposal:
> "I want to make sure this makes sense before either of us invests more time. Based on our call — is this the direction you wanted to go, or do I need to adjust anything?"

### After Verbal Yes
Move fast. Delay kills deals.
1. Send contract within 24 hours (use `execution/send_email.py` + contract template in `documents/`)
2. Request 50% deposit to confirm start date
3. Book kickoff call for the week they confirm

## Objection Handling

The goal when handling an objection isn't to win an argument — it's to understand the real concern and address it honestly. Use their words in your response, not scripted counters.

| Objection | What's Behind It | Response |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| "Too expensive" | Unclear ROI | "What's 5 hours/week worth over a year? At $50/hr that's $13K in admin time. This pays for itself in 2 months. Want me to run the math for your situation?" |
| "We're too small" | Unsure it applies to them | "Small teams actually benefit most — you don't have an ops person to absorb this work. Automation IS your ops team." |
| "We tried automation before" | Bad past experience | "What broke? Usually it's the implementation, not the idea. We build around your specific workflow — what went wrong last time?" |
| "Let me think about it" | Unresolved concern | "Totally fair. What's the biggest thing on your mind right now? I'd rather address it directly than have you sit on a question." |
| "We don't have budget right now" | Timing or priority | "Understood. When does your budget cycle reset? And what would need to be true for this to make the list?" |
| "We're evaluating other options" | Hasn't differentiated you | "Makes sense. What matters most to you in making this decision? I want to make sure you're comparing apples to apples." |
