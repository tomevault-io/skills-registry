# Awareness — Pragmatic Framework Skill

## What This Skill Does

This skill helps you develop **Awareness** programs as defined by the Pragmatic Institute Framework. The Awareness activity lives in the **Programs** category of the framework and is defined as:

> Develop programs to raise the profile and awareness of your brand within strategic market segments to bring more prospects into your funnel.

The output is a strategic awareness program plan that connects upstream market knowledge (positioning, buyer personas, market problems, competitive landscape) to top-of-funnel program execution — content, thought leadership, advertising, events, and PR — designed to make your brand the first one prospects think of when they recognize a need in your category.

## Framework Context

Awareness sits in the **Programs** category alongside Marketing Plan, Revenue Growth, Revenue Retention, Launch, Nurturing, Advocacy, and Measurement. It is the top-of-funnel counterpart to Nurturing (which moves prospects through the funnel) and feeds directly into the broader Marketing Plan.

**Key upstream dependencies:**
- **Positioning** — Provides the messaging pillars and problem-solution framing that awareness content must communicate
- **Buyer Personas** — Defines who you are trying to reach, their roles, watering holes, and information preferences
- **Market Definition** — Identifies the strategic segments where awareness investment should concentrate
- **Competitive Landscape** — Reveals where differentiation opportunities exist and where you must establish share of voice
- **Buyer Experience** — Maps the earliest stages of the buyer journey where awareness programs must operate
- **Marketing Plan** — The strategic umbrella that allocates budget, sets goals, and schedules awareness programs within the fiscal period

**Key downstream connections:**
- **Nurturing** — Awareness brings prospects into the funnel; Nurturing moves them through it
- **Measurement** — Awareness programs must be measured and tuned for alignment with corporate goals
- **Revenue Growth** — Effective awareness feeds the pipeline that Revenue Growth plans depend on
- **Content** (Enablement) — Awareness programs generate and consume content assets

## Roles and Responsibilities

Per the Pragmatic Framework Roles & Responsibilities document, the Awareness activity requires:

**Responsibilities:**
- Create and deliver thoughtful information to influence key market constituents
- Influence key audiences via strong content
- Deliver presentations to internal and external audiences

**Required Skills:**
- Written and verbal communications
- Pitch artist capabilities
- Storytelling
- Ability to build street credibility
- Strong understanding of the industry, the market, and market problems

## Workflow

Follow these six steps to build a complete Awareness program plan. Load reference files as indicated at each step.

### Step 1: Audit Strategic Inputs
> Load `references/input-audit.md` for the detailed checklist.

Before designing awareness programs, validate that the necessary upstream artifacts exist and are current. The most critical inputs are Positioning (messaging pillars by persona), Buyer Personas (target audience profiles), Market Definition (priority segments), and Competitive Landscape (share of voice baseline). Identify gaps and determine whether you can proceed with assumptions or must pause to complete upstream work.

### Step 2: Define Awareness Objectives and Audience
> Load `references/objectives-audience.md` for guidance on goal-setting and audience targeting.

Establish what "awareness" means for your situation — unaided recall, aided recognition, share of voice, category association, or thought leadership authority. Define specific, measurable objectives tied to business outcomes. Then map your target audience by segment and persona, identifying the channels and watering holes where they consume information.

### Step 3: Develop the Awareness Strategy
> Load `references/awareness-strategy.md` for the strategic framework.

Build the strategic approach that connects your positioning to your target audiences through the right mix of program types. This includes selecting the awareness model (brand-led vs. problem-led vs. thought-leadership-led), defining the content pillars derived from positioning, choosing program types (content marketing, PR/analyst relations, advertising, events, social, community), and establishing the messaging hierarchy that translates positioning into awareness-stage communications.

### Step 4: Design the Program Portfolio
> Load `references/program-portfolio.md` for program types, channel selection, and tactical planning.

Translate the strategy into a concrete portfolio of awareness programs — each with defined objectives, target personas, channels, content requirements, timing, and budget. Programs should span owned, earned, and paid channels and should be mapped to the buyer experience stages where awareness-level engagement occurs. Include both always-on programs (SEO, content hub, social presence) and campaign-based programs (launches, events, seasonal pushes).

### Step 5: Establish Measurement Framework
> Load `references/measurement.md` for metrics hierarchy and tracking guidance.

Define how you will measure awareness program effectiveness at three levels: activity metrics (what you did), engagement metrics (how the market responded), and outcome metrics (business impact). Include both leading indicators (reach, impressions, share of voice) and lagging indicators (unaided recall, pipeline contribution, MQL volume from awareness sources). Set up the feedback loop that connects measurement back to program optimization.

### Step 6: Assemble and Review
> Load `templates/awareness-program-plan.md` for the plan document template.
> Load `templates/program-brief.md` for individual program briefs.

Compile the complete awareness program plan document. Review against the common pitfalls checklist, validate alignment with the Marketing Plan and Positioning, and confirm stakeholder buy-in. Produce individual program briefs for each major awareness initiative.

## Output Formats

This skill can produce:
1. **Awareness Program Plan** — Comprehensive strategic document covering objectives, audience, strategy, program portfolio, measurement, and calendar
2. **Individual Program Briefs** — Tactical briefs for each awareness initiative
3. **Awareness Audit** — Assessment of current awareness posture with gap analysis
4. **Measurement Dashboard Specification** — KPI definitions, data sources, and reporting cadence

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Inside-out messaging** — Building awareness around product features instead of market problems the audience cares about
2. **Segment-blind programs** — Running generic awareness campaigns instead of targeting strategic segments identified in Market Definition
3. **Persona-deaf content** — Creating content that doesn't match how specific buyer personas consume information or what they care about
4. **Activity over outcome** — Measuring what you produced (blog posts, webinars) rather than what the market did in response (engagement, recall, pipeline)
5. **Awareness-nurturing conflation** — Blurring the line between top-of-funnel awareness (brand visibility) and mid-funnel nurturing (lead progression), leading to programs that try to do both and accomplish neither
6. **Channel-first thinking** — Starting with "we need a LinkedIn strategy" instead of "where do our target personas go to learn about problems like ours"
7. **Ignoring the 95-5 rule** — Focusing only on the ~5% of buyers who are in-market now while neglecting the 95% who will buy later and need to remember your brand when they do
8. **No competitive context** — Measuring awareness in isolation without tracking share of voice relative to competitors
