# Perfume to Modern Minimalist Prompt

## Overview

This skill transforms perfume descriptions into detailed prompts for image generation AI, specifically crafted to evoke the aesthetic of modern minimalist design and photography. The output prompts guide AI to create images with characteristic clean lines, abundant negative space, neutral color palettes, geometric forms, architectural shadows, and the refined sophistication of contemporary high-end perfume brands.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Converting perfume descriptions or fragrance notes into visual art prompts
- Creating image prompts in modern minimalist style (2010s-2020s)
- Requesting artwork that captures the essence of a scent through clean, refined aesthetics
- Needing prompts for unisex, citrus, clean, musky, or woody fragrances
- Wanting sophisticated, urban, contemporary visual representations
- Evoking brands like Byredo, Le Labo, Aesop, COS, or minimalist Scandinavian design

## Prompt Generation Process

### Step 1: Analyze the Perfume Description

Extract key elements from the perfume description:
- **Top notes**: Initial, lighter scent impressions
- **Middle notes**: Heart of the fragrance
- **Base notes**: Deep, lasting impressions
- **Mood/atmosphere**: Emotional qualities (clean, sophisticated, urban, serene, refined)
- **Visual imagery**: Any existing visual associations or metaphors

### Step 2: Translate Scent to Visual Elements

Map fragrance characteristics to minimalist visual components:

**Color palette:**
- Clean/Citrus notes → Pure white, soft cream, pale gray, hint of pale yellow or green
- Woody notes → Warm beige, taupe, light oak, soft brown, natural wood tones
- Musky notes → Off-white, pearl gray, soft stone, alabaster
- Fresh/Aquatic → Cool gray, soft blue-gray, white, silver
- Green notes → Sage green, soft olive, pale mint, concrete gray
- Warm/Amber → Warm beige, sand, soft gold, ivory

**Compositional elements:**
- Light fragrances → Maximum negative space, minimal elements, high-key lighting
- Complex fragrances → Layered geometric forms, subtle complexity, architectural structure
- Clean fragrances → Pure backgrounds, single focal elements, pristine surfaces

**Objects and symbols:**
- Use abstract geometric forms rather than literal ingredients
- Glass vessels (cylindrical, spherical, rectangular)
- Stone or concrete elements
- Architectural shadows and light
- Negative space as primary compositional element
- Minimalist botanical elements (single stem, leaf silhouette)

### Step 3: Apply Modern Minimalist Techniques

Incorporate specific technical elements from contemporary minimalist design:

**Lighting (Critical):**
- **Soft diffused studio lighting**: Even, shadowless illumination for clean aesthetic
- **Architectural shadows**: Hard-edged geometric shadows from window light
- **Gradient lighting**: Subtle gradations from light to shadow
- **Backlighting**: Rim light creating silhouettes and transparency
- **Natural window light**: Soft directional light with geometric shadow patterns

**Compositional style:**
- **Negative space dominance**: 60-80% empty space in composition
- **Rule of thirds**: Minimal elements placed strategically
- **Geometric balance**: Clean lines, right angles, perfect circles
- **Layered depth**: Foreground, middle, background separation with minimal elements
- **Asymmetrical minimalism**: Off-center placement for sophistication

**Material and texture:**
- **Glass**: Transparency, refraction, geometric vessels
- **Stone/Concrete**: Matte texture, monolithic forms, architectural quality
- **Wood**: Natural grain, warm minimalism, Scandinavian aesthetic
- **Metal**: Brushed aluminum, matte steel, refined industrial
- **Fabric**: Linen, cotton, natural fibers in neutral tones

**Color characteristics:**
- **Neutral palette dominance**: White, gray, beige, black as primary colors
- **Monochromatic schemes**: Single color in multiple tones
- **Subtle accent**: One muted color as accent (sage, dusty blue, warm beige)
- **High-key or low-key**: Predominantly light OR predominantly dark, not mixed
- **Matte finishes**: Avoid glossy or saturated colors

**Photographic quality:**
- **Shallow depth of field**: Selective focus, soft background blur
- **High resolution detail**: Sharp, crisp where in focus
- **Studio photography aesthetic**: Controlled, professional lighting
- **Editorial quality**: Magazine-worthy composition and execution
- **Contemporary camera work**: Modern digital photography feel, not vintage

## Modern Minimalist Style Reference

### Key Aesthetic References

**Byredo (Swedish minimalism):**
- Extreme simplicity and restraint
- Neutral palette with occasional muted accent
- Geometric bottles and clean surfaces
- Abundant negative space
- Sophisticated urban aesthetic
- Best for: Unisex, clean, sophisticated fragrances

**Le Labo (Artisanal minimalism):**
- Natural materials (wood, kraft paper, amber glass)
- Warm minimalism with texture
- Hand-crafted industrial aesthetic
- Understated luxury
- Neutral earth tones
- Best for: Woody, musky, artisanal fragrances

**Aesop (Apothecary minimalism):**
- Amber and brown glass vessels
- Clinical-meets-natural aesthetic
- Botanical minimalism
- Intellectual sophistication
- Warm neutrals with dark accents
- Best for: Botanical, herbal, refined fragrances

**Kinfolk/Cereal Magazine Style:**
- Soft natural light
- Abundant negative space
- Neutral linens and natural materials
- Lifestyle minimalism
- Warm, inviting restraint
- Best for: Natural, lifestyle-oriented fragrances

**Norm Architects/Scandinavian Design:**
- Pure forms and honest materials
- Light wood and white surfaces
- Architectural precision
- Serene, meditative quality
- Natural light emphasis
- Best for: Clean, fresh, serene fragrances

**Contemporary Art Gallery Aesthetic:**
- White cube spaces
- Dramatic architectural shadows
- Sculptural object placement
- Conceptual minimalism
- High contrast or all-white
- Best for: Artistic, conceptual, sophisticated fragrances

## Guidelines for Effective Prompts

1. **Emphasize negative space** - 60-80% empty space is crucial for minimalist aesthetic
2. **Specify neutral palette** - White, gray, beige, black with minimal accent colors
3. **Define geometric forms** - Use clear geometric shapes (cylinder, sphere, rectangle, plane)
4. **Describe lighting precisely** - Soft diffused OR architectural shadows, not both simultaneously
5. **Specify materials** - Glass, concrete, wood, stone, metal with surface qualities
6. **Avoid literal representation** - Use abstract geometric forms rather than actual perfume ingredients
7. **Reference contemporary brands** - Byredo, Le Labo, Aesop anchor the aesthetic clearly
8. **Indicate photographic quality** - Studio photography, editorial quality, shallow depth of field
9. **Emphasize restraint** - "Less is more" philosophy throughout
10. **Specify mood through space** - Use negative space and light to create atmosphere

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Don't overcrowd the composition - minimalism requires restraint
- Avoid bright or saturated colors - stick to neutral, muted tones
- Don't use decorative or ornamental elements - keep it pure and simple
- Avoid realistic shading from painting - this is photographic realism with minimal subjects
- Don't include too many objects - one to three elements maximum
- Avoid busy backgrounds - clean, simple, or gradient only
- Don't mix warm and cool lighting - choose one temperature and maintain it
- Avoid vintage or aged aesthetics - this is contemporary and pristine
- Don't use literal botanical elements unless extremely simplified - prefer geometric abstraction
- Avoid explaining or decorating - let the space and form speak

## Additional Customization Options

Users can request variations by specifying:
- Lighting style (soft diffused, architectural shadows, gradient, backlit)
- Color temperature (warm minimalism vs cool minimalism)
- Material emphasis (glass, concrete, wood, stone, metal)
- Compositional format (square, vertical, horizontal, panoramic)
- Level of abstraction (purely geometric vs minimal natural elements)
- Mood emphasis (clean and fresh, warm and sophisticated, cool and urban, serene and meditative)
- Brand aesthetic reference (Byredo, Le Labo, Aesop, COS, etc.)
- Amount of negative space (60-90%)

## Perfume-to-Style Matching Guide

**Best for Modern Minimalism:**
- Clean, fresh fragrances (citrus, white tea, soap)
- Unisex fragrances (woody, musky, aromatic)
- Sophisticated woody fragrances (cedar, sandalwood, vetiver)
- White/clean musk fragrances
- Modern aquatic or ozone fragrances
- Urban, contemporary fragrances

**Can work with adaptation:**
- Oriental fragrances (use warm minimalism, amber glass, darker tones)
- Floral fragrances (use single stem silhouette, extreme simplification)
- Green fragrances (use sage tones, botanical minimalism)

**Less suitable:**
- Heavy gourmand fragrances (too literal and sweet for minimalist aesthetic)
- Vintage-style classic fragrances (require different aesthetic approach)
- Extremely complex or baroque fragrances (minimalism works better with clarity)

Always ask clarifying questions if the perfume description is minimal or if the desired artistic direction is unclear.
